Bio

I am a final-year MSCS student at Stanford University. My academic interests lie in the intersection between AI and cybersecurity, particularly the privacy and security implications of recent AI technologies. I am also broadly interested in cryptography and how technology can be used to combat climate change and economic inequality. I am doing research at Stanford Trustworthy AI Research (STAIR) with Prof. Sanmi Koyejo, AI for Climate Change with Prof. Andrew Ng, and working on Cybench: a benchmark for evaluating the cybersecurity capabilities and risk of language models with Profs. Percy Liang, Dan Boneh, and Daniel Ho.

I received my undergraduate degree from Cornell University in 2022, double majoring in Mathematics and Computer Science. At Cornell, I was advised by Prof. Joe Halpern and did research on post-quantum cryptography with Prof. Noah Stephens-Davidowitz. I was also a junior inductee into the Phi Beta Kappa honors society, an honor reserved for the top 3% of the graduating class.

I am from Bangkok, Thailand, but am currently based in Stanford, California.
